The Chapel of Saint Sebastian

You stand within the only standing structure of the once proud Monastery.
Evidently the chapel is looked after for the floors are swept, pews wiped
and the stained-glass windows in good working order. A modest pulpit
made of mahogany with a carved pine cross attached to it faces a few rows
of wooden benches. Probably cared for by questing paladins or clerics
on a pilgrimage, you wonder if any of the faithful might be nearby. To
the west wall you see a hole large enough for you to squeeze through.
