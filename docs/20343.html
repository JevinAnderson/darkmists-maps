Bank of Thera

The first thing you notice when you enter this branch of the Bank of Thera is
the row and column panel covering the back wall, west of you. There are a
number of squares set in rows and columns on a panel of shimmering mithril.
Sconces on tripod stands hold flaming torches. In the light of their flames,
a smooth, striated stone counter gleams, its brown and grey surface smooth.
The walls and ceiling are of the same polished, striated stone. Just to the
north of the counter is a marble statue, its alabaster hue in contrast with
the natural stone surroundings.
