The Tower Library

Perched atop the Hourglass Tower, the view here of the surrounding
forest is spectacular. The floor was cut from a single massive sliver
of clear quartz, not an imperfection nor streak can be seen upon it.
Framing the circular floor are several free-floating railings of similar
translucent mineral, keeping any preoccupied visitors from tumbling over
the side. Several gently humming bookcases slowly rotate in a deliberate
pattern, as though floating on an unseen carousel. The domed ceiling is
etched with grooves on the outside to allow for any precipitation to roll
off easily in miniature waterfalls. Blue, white and black columns rise to
the center of ceiling and downward, giving support to a spiral stairway.
A strange and dark cloud hovers at the center of the dome, revealing the
occasional geometric lightning.
